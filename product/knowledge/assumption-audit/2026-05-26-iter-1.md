# Assumption audit — Loreum Chamber (iteration 1)

## Health context

Corpus health is now **developing**: 8 sources across research, ticket, and memo
types. Three new competitive-research artifacts (Safe/Zodiac, Aragon/Snapshot,
Colony/Hats/ERC-8004) triangulate the **Safe+Zodiac incumbent stack** from
multiple vendor/docs sources — still **no user interviews**. Single-source remains
common for individual competitor claims. Totals: **6 assumptions** and **~40
single-source claims**.

## Assumptions (zero provenance)

### High

1. **Treasury teams prefer market-driven NFT boards over fixed signers.**
   - Decision at risk: positioning vs Safe/Tally/Aragon modular token governance.
   - Source: internal memos only; new research shows Aragon, Colony, Hats all
     offer alternative "who leads" models without NFT delegation leaderboards.

2. **Organizations want AI agents as first-class directors.**
   - Decision at risk: agent deployment, ValidationRegistry, auto-confirm policies.
   - Source: `chamber-product-intent.md`; ERC-8004 research confirms agent
     identity layer exists but not buyer demand for director seats.

3. **Chamber can win without a module/plugin ecosystem near term.**
   - Decision at risk: build vs integrate Zodiac-like composability.
   - Source: competitive gap; **counter-evidence**: Karpatkey, Tally, Aragon all
     compose deeply with Safe+Zodiac — buyers may expect module composability.

4. **Target buyers will prefer native onchain execution over Snapshot+Safe bridging.** *(new)*
   - Decision at risk: whether Chamber's queue is a differentiator buyers pay for.
   - Source: inferred from Snapshot execution-gap pain + Chamber intent; **no buyer
     quote** that they switched for this reason.

### Medium

5. **ERC-8004 alignment materially influences buyer choice.**
   - Decision at risk: marketing vs treasury UX priorities.
   - Source: analyst recommendation; EIP-8004 explicitly disclaims capability
     guarantees — composable infra, not procurement driver by itself.

6. **Stakeholders understand delegation-to-NFT-ID mechanics.**
   - Decision at risk: activation; P1 UX finding on raw token ID entry.

## Triage recommendation

Validate **#3 (module ecosystem)** and **#4 (execution gap differentiation)**
first — new research strengthens the Safe+Zodiac incumbent narrative, which
directly challenges Chamber's "no modules yet" gap. Run 3–5 director/treasury

The team will plan against whatever it does not question. These are the things to question first.
